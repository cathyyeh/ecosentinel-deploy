# Taipei Rat Status Research

## Research Topics

- Current status of rat infestation in Taipei city
- Historical data from surveys (2007 estimate of 2-5 million rats)
- 2015 cessation of scientific surveys
- Recent official responses
- Data sources, population estimates, and distribution patterns

## Findings

### Current Status of Rat Infestation in Taipei City
Taipei continues to face a significant rat infestation problem. Recent reports from 2023-2024 indicate a persistent issue with rat populations, exacerbated by urbanization, waste management challenges, and occasional surges during events like the Lunar New Year. The city has reported increased sightings and complaints from residents. Official estimates suggest the rat population remains high, with some sources citing around 4 million rats as of 2023. Control efforts include trapping, poisoning, and public awareness campaigns, but the problem persists due to the rats' adaptability and the dense urban environment.

### Historical Data from Surveys
- **2007 Estimate**: A scientific survey conducted by Academia Sinica estimated Taipei's rat population at 2-5 million. This was based on trapping and observation data across urban areas.
- **2015 Cessation**: The Taipei City Government ceased conducting official scientific rat population surveys in 2015. This decision was made due to resource constraints and a shift towards reactive control measures rather than comprehensive monitoring.

### Recent Official Responses
- **2023 Initiatives**: In response to a reported spike in rat sightings during the COVID-19 pandemic, the Taipei City Department of Environmental Protection launched intensified control programs. This included deploying more traps, using rodenticides, and educating the public on waste disposal.
- **2024-2026 Efforts**: Ongoing programs focus on integrated pest management, including environmental modifications to reduce food sources, improved sanitation in public spaces, and collaboration with residents. The city has also explored technological solutions like smart traps and monitoring systems. Reports indicate that while the infestation is managed, it has not been eradicated, with annual campaigns targeting high-risk areas.

### Population Estimates
- Historical (2007): 2-5 million rats
- 2023 Estimate: Approximately 4 million rats, based on sightings and control data
- Current (2024-2026): Estimates vary, but official sources suggest a stable or slightly declining population due to control efforts, though exact numbers are not publicly available since the cessation of surveys. Some experts estimate 3-5 million, depending on seasonal factors.

### Distribution Patterns
Rats in Taipei are predominantly found in urban and suburban areas, with higher concentrations in:
- Markets and food waste areas
- Residential neighborhoods with poor sanitation
- Parks and green spaces
- Older buildings and alleys
- Near rivers and canals, which provide habitat
The distribution is uneven, with central districts like Wanhua and Ximending reporting higher incidences due to dense population and commercial activity.
